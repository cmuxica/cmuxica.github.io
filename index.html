Christian J. Muxica

cmuxica [at] g [dot] ucla [dot] edu

I am a second year PhD student at UCLA in the department of linguistics. My interests are primarily in semantics, pragmatics, and psycholinguistics. I study the mental representations underlying meaning in natural language and the process by which such representations are assembled in real-time from spoken and written input. I have secondary interests in intonation, acquisition, and computational linguistics as well.

In my research, I employ formal tools from theoretical linguistics and experimental methodologies such as eyetracking and pupilometry. I am primarily advised by Jesse Harris and I am associated with the language processing lab here. Currently, I am investigating the representation of focus alternatives during comprehension.

Before coming to Los Angeles, I was a Lab Manager for the Cognitive Science of Language Lab at UMass Amherst where I earned my undergraduate degree. In my free time I like to make music, climb rocks, and take pictures.
